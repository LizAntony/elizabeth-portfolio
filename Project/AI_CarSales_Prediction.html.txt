    <h1>AI‑Driven Car Sales Prediction</h1>
    <p class="tagline">
      Comparing Decision Tree, KNN, and Regression models to forecast car sales using AI.
    </p>

    <h2>Project Overview</h2>
    <p>
      This project explores how <strong>AI and machine‑learning models</strong> can be used to predict monthly car sales
      based on a combination of marketing, economic, and competitive variables. I designed the workflow to compare
      multiple models and identify the approach that delivers the lowest prediction error.
    </p>

    <p>
      The dataset includes variables that are positively and negatively correlated with sales. Advertising spend and
      economic strength tend to increase sales, while competitor discounts reduce them. After preparing the data, I
      trained three models: a <strong>Decision Tree Regressor</strong>, a <strong>K‑Nearest Neighbors (KNN)</strong>
      model, and a <strong>Linear Regression</strong> model.
    </p>

    <h2>Sample Dataset</h2>
    <p>The cleaned dataset used for modeling is structured as follows:</p>

    <p>
      <strong>Positively correlated:</strong> AdvertisingSpend, EconomicIndex<br>

    </p>

    <h2>AI Modeling and Evaluation</h2>
    <p>
      I trained three models to predict <strong>CarSales</strong> from the other variables and evaluated them using
      <strong>Mean Squared Error (MSE)</strong>:
    </p>

    <div class="code-block">
      Decision Tree MSE:  &lt;lowest&gt;<br>
      KNN MSE:            higher<br>
      Linear Regression MSE: intermediate
    </div>

    <p>
      The <strong>Decision Tree</strong> achieved the lowest MSE, indicating that it captured the non‑linear
      relationships in the data more effectively than KNN or Linear Regression. This makes it the recommended model
      for forecasting car sales in this scenario.
    </p>

    <h2>What I Learned</h2>
    <p>
      This project reinforced how important data preparation, feature selection, and model evaluation are in any
      AI‑driven workflow. Even with a relatively small dataset, the choice of model and error metric significantly
      affects the quality of predictions. The Decision Tree model’s performance highlighted the value of capturing
      non‑linear interactions between variables.
    </p>

    <p>
      More broadly, this work reflects how I think about AI: as a tool that augments human judgment. The model can
      quickly generate forecasts, but the real value comes from interpreting those predictions thoughtfully and
      embedding them in a broader decision‑making context, supported by clear visualizations in tools like Power BI.
    </p>
